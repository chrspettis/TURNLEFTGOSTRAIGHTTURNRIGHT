Your Story Scroll

Write 10 sentences total using this pattern: Direction + travel report + destination name.

Assignment: Unit 3 Lesson 2.2 — Receptive/Expressive: Talking about Directions

Due: Monday, January 19, 2026

Student Instructions

WHAT IS THIS GAME?
This is a direction-choice game for fingerspelling practice. Each turn, you choose a path and arrive at a new destination.

HOW DO YOU PLAY IT?

  1. Read the three Street Signs (the preview cards).
  2. Choose ONE direction button.
  3. Read the Travel Report (what happened on your way).
  4. Fingerspell the destination name exactly as written.
  5. Write 1–2 sentences per move in your Story Scroll assignment.

FINGERSPELLING RULES

  • Stay in the box — Hand at chest level near your dominant shoulder.
  • Freeze your arm — Elbow/arm steady. Only the hand changes.
  • Clean letters only — Make each letter clearly.
  • No bouncing — Most letters are still. Only J and Z move.
  • Smooth speed — Go steady. If someone looks confused, slow down.
  • Don’t twist — Keep your palm facing the right way.
  • Double letters show twice — Small repeat for “ll,” “ss,” etc.
  • Eyes up — Look at the person/camera, not your own hand.
